andalusie

In October 2017 we traveled for two weeks around Andalusia, part of southern Spain. We visited couple of main cities and one National park.

We flew from Prague straight to Malaga, where we stayed for a few days in Airbnb room. Malaga is the second largest city of Andalusia, after Sevilla. Most parts of Malaga are nice and clean. There is a huge beach along the whole city. Probably the main monument there is Alcazaba, palatial fortification, built in the early 11th century. Pablo Picasso was born in Malaga, so we also visited Picasso Museum.

After a few days we took BlaBlaCar to Granada, where we planed to stay for two days only, but we liked it so much so we booked another night. On the southeast you can see Siera Nevada mountains and closer to the city on the east site there is an Alhambra fortress surrounded by very nice hills. So Granada has this feeling of the mountain city.

In Alhambra we visited only free areas, because for the main parts you have to reserve your ticket in advance and we wanted to be more spontaneous and didn't know what day we'll be there. The architecture was nice but there was a lot of turists. We enjoyed more the walk on the hills around Alhambra, where we walked alone throw the olive wood.

We really liked one coffee shop called Café Baraka and went there every day. They had good coffee and amazing pancakes. After some time during this trip we found out it's better to order 'cafe con leche' (coffee with milk) then capuccino. We like it better and it's usually cheaper.

Our next stop after Granada was Córdoba, main city of former Al-Andalus, muslim territory. Muslims conquered a significant part of Spain in the 8th century and have left in 15th century. So you can see elements of Islamic architecture throughout Andalusia.

We visited Great Mezquita or Mosque–Cathedral of Córdoba early in the morning because there are a lot less turists there. It use to be medieval mezquita originally, then the Christians built in the cathedral in 16th century, so now we can see quite interesting mix.
We also went to see Alcázar of Córdoba, Castle of the Christian Monarchs. We red all this reviews tells us it's amazing and that we should delete all our pics from camera to have some room for this beauty. It was nice, but maybe we were already so overhelmed by all this other stuff so it wasn't simply so great as we expected.

For me Córdoba was somehow cleaner and more tidy then Granada and I actually didn't appreciate it. But it was really nice still.
