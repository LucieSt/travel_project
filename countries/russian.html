MOSCOW

We visited Moscow in November 2014. It was quite cold there.

Our first stop was Budapest, where we went from Prague by RegioJet bus. It was my first time there and I loved the city. It reminded me of Prague but bigger, even the subway is quite similar. We spend one sunny afternoon there and the next day early in the morning flew to Moscow.

When we got out of the airport we instantly noticed how cold there is. We booked our hostel in advance and when we got there, they told us only rooms without window is available. We should stay in the city for 6 days so we wanted the window. The receptionist was not very helpful, so we cancel our order and found another hostel, where was an offer of discounted room for six persons with the window.

In the first few days we visited the most famous places like Red Square with Saint Basil's Cathedral, Lenin's mausoleum and GUM store. Mausoleum was under the reconstruction so we couldn't go inside. We were inside the Saint Basil's Cathedral and also at GUM.

Moscow has this cool or freezy feeling.

Because it was October and was already cold outside, we decided to explore the city subway.

The construction of subway in Moscow is similar to the Prague one, because we inspired in Russian. Both go deep into the ground, have this "hall" type platforms etc. Subway in Moscow has many individual lines and tvo round ones. The ticket for subway doesn't have a time limit, it is valid until you come up from the subway again, so this was a cheap amusement.

We visited maybe ten prettiest stations there are according to some article. I found interesting that station called Pražskaja was build by czech ingeneers and conversely our station Anděl, former Moskevská, was build by russian ingeneers. You can see the similarities of lighting in Dostoyevskaya station in Moscow and Anděl.
